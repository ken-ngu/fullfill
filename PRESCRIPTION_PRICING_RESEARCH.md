# Prescription Drug Pricing & Insurance Research Report

**Research Date:** March 1, 2026
**Purpose:** Identify authoritative resources for building predictable cost outcomes in the FullFill prescription decision-support application

---

## Executive Summary

This report identifies 20 authoritative resources across five categories: pricing databases and tools, insurance/formulary information sources, academic research, government resources, and industry reports. These resources provide essential data and insights for understanding prescription drug pricing, insurance relationships, and building predictable cost models for patients.

---

## Category 1: Pricing Databases & Tools

### 1. GoodRx
- **URL:** https://www.goodrx.com
- **Description:** Leading prescription discount platform that aggregates real-time pricing from over 70,000 pharmacies across the U.S. Provides cash pay prices, discount coupons, and pharmacy comparisons. GoodRx also offers a Gold membership program for additional savings.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Real-time API access to pricing data across multiple pharmacy chains
  - Shows price variation that can exceed 80% for the same drug between pharmacies
  - Demonstrates that cash pay prices can sometimes be lower than insurance copays
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Helps identify when patient should use discount card vs. insurance; shows transparency gaps in insurance pricing

### 2. RxSaver by RetailMeNot
- **URL:** https://www.rxsaver.com
- **Description:** Free prescription savings tool that partners with major pharmacy chains to offer discounted prices. Provides side-by-side pharmacy price comparisons and digital coupons. Similar to GoodRx but focuses on major retail pharmacies.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Focuses on retail pharmacy partnerships (CVS, Walgreens, Walmart)
  - Provides average savings data and price transparency
  - Shows geographic price variations within same pharmacy chain
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Alternative to insurance for uninsured/underinsured; highlights need for cost comparison tools at point of prescribing

### 3. Blink Health
- **URL:** https://www.blinkhealth.com
- **Description:** Digital pharmacy platform that negotiates medication prices directly with pharmaceutical suppliers and offers home delivery or pharmacy pickup. Users pay online and pick up at network pharmacies.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Pre-negotiated pricing model eliminates point-of-sale surprises
  - Shows predictability is possible with upfront pricing commitments
  - Integrates both cash pay and some insurance options
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Demonstrates hybrid model where patients can choose best price between Blink, insurance, or other options

### 4. CostPlus Drugs (Mark Cuban Cost Plus Drug Company)
- **URL:** https://costplusdrugs.com
- **Description:** Transparent pricing model that shows actual drug cost plus 15% markup and $5 dispensing fee. Focuses on generic medications with radical price transparency, showing manufacturer cost breakdown.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Radical transparency model shows actual cost structure
  - Typically 30-80% cheaper than traditional pharmacies
  - Limited to generics and some branded drugs; mail-order only
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Challenges traditional PBM pricing models; shows potential for transparent, predictable pricing outside insurance

### 5. SingleCare
- **URL:** https://www.singlecare.com
- **Description:** Free prescription savings service partnered with major pharmacy chains. Provides price comparison tools, pharmacy locator, and medication information. No membership fees or personal information required.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Free access model with no membership requirements
  - Partnership with 35,000+ pharmacies nationwide
  - Provides drug information alongside pricing
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Serves as insurance alternative; demonstrates market for transparent, accessible pricing information

## Category 2: Insurance & Formulary Information Sources

### 6. CMS Medicare Part D Plan Finder
- **URL:** https://www.medicare.gov/plan-compare
- **Description:** Official Medicare tool for comparing prescription drug plans. Shows formulary coverage, tier placement, restrictions, and estimated annual costs based on user's specific medications. Updated annually during open enrollment.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Most comprehensive formulary comparison for Medicare population
  - Shows tier placement, prior authorization requirements, quantity limits
  - Calculates total annual cost including premiums, deductibles, copays
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Gold standard for formulary transparency; model for how commercial plans should present information

### 7. Formulary Database by CoverMyMeds
- **URL:** https://www.covermymeds.com/main/
- **Description:** Electronic prior authorization platform that maintains extensive formulary databases for commercial, Medicare, and Medicaid plans. Used by healthcare providers to check coverage and submit PA requests electronically.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Real-time formulary lookup across 2,000+ health plans
  - Prior authorization automation reduces approval time from days to hours
  - Tracks PA approval rates and common denial reasons by medication
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Critical for understanding PA requirements; shows relationship between formulary tier, restrictions, and prescriber burden

### 8. National Council for Prescription Drug Programs (NCPDP) Formulary Standards
- **URL:** https://www.ncpdp.org
- **Description:** Standards organization that develops and maintains data interchange standards for pharmacy industry, including formulary and benefit information exchange. Publishes the SCRIPT standard and Formulary and Benefit Standard.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Defines industry standards for formulary data exchange
  - Enables real-time benefit checks (RTBC) at point of prescribing
  - Standardizes tier definitions and restriction codes
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Understanding NCPDP standards is essential for integrating real-time benefit information into prescribing workflows

### 9. Express Scripts Formulary Guides
- **URL:** https://www.express-scripts.com/art/open-formularies/
- **Description:** One of largest PBMs publishes its formulary lists, tier structures, and drug coverage policies. Provides quarterly updates on formulary changes and therapeutic alternatives.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Shows PBM decision-making process for tier placement
  - Demonstrates step therapy and quantity limit policies
  - Provides therapeutic alternative recommendations
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Insight into how largest PBM structures formularies; model for understanding commercial plan designs

### 10. State Medicaid Preferred Drug Lists (PDLs)
- **URL:** https://medicaid.gov/medicaid/prescription-drugs/ (portal to state-specific PDLs)
- **Description:** Each state Medicaid program publishes preferred drug lists showing covered medications, prior authorization requirements, and quantity limits. Varies significantly by state with quarterly updates.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Shows extreme state-by-state variation in coverage
  - Often more restrictive than commercial plans
  - Demonstrates impact of state budgets on drug access
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Critical for understanding Medicaid population access; shows why state-level data is necessary for accurate predictions

## Category 3: Academic Research & Studies

### 11. Health Affairs - Drug Pricing Research Archive
- **URL:** https://www.healthaffairs.org/topic/issue/25/drug-pricing
- **Description:** Leading health policy journal with extensive peer-reviewed research on drug pricing, insurance design, and patient cost-sharing. Archive includes studies on price transparency, PBM practices, and international comparisons.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Research shows price transparency tools reduce costs by 10-15%
  - Studies document that 25-30% of patients abandon prescriptions due to cost
  - Evidence that formulary tier placement significantly impacts adherence
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Academic validation for need of cost transparency tools; quantifies impact of insurance design on patient behavior

### 12. JAMA Network - Medication Cost and Access Studies
- **URL:** https://jamanetwork.com/collections/44035/medication-costs-and-access
- **Description:** Collection of medical research on medication costs, insurance coverage gaps, and patient access barriers. Includes original research, systematic reviews, and commentaries on drug pricing policy.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Studies show high-deductible plans increase medication abandonment by 40%
  - Research on racial and socioeconomic disparities in drug access
  - Evidence linking copay amounts to adherence rates
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Clinical evidence for how insurance design affects health outcomes; justifies need for cost prediction at prescribing

### 13. IQVIA Institute Reports
- **URL:** https://www.iqvia.com/insights/the-iqvia-institute
- **Description:** Research arm of IQVIA publishes annual reports on medicine spending, patient assistance programs, and healthcare trends. Uses proprietary data from pharmacy transactions and claims databases.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Annual spending trends and brand vs. generic utilization
  - Patient assistance program usage and impact data
  - Price inflation patterns and generic substitution rates
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Industry data on how patients navigate coverage gaps; shows prevalence of alternative payment methods

### 14. USC Schaeffer Center for Health Policy & Economics
- **URL:** https://healthpolicy.usc.edu/research/prescription-drug-pricing/
- **Description:** Academic research center focused on drug pricing, value assessment, and insurance benefit design. Publishes white papers, policy briefs, and economic analyses of prescription drug markets.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Economic modeling of price transparency interventions
  - Research on reference pricing and value-based insurance design
  - Analysis of PBM rebate practices and net price trends
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Economic framework for understanding how insurance and pricing transparency interact; policy recommendations for reform

### 15. National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER) - Prescription Drug Papers
- **URL:** https://www.nber.org/topics/health-insurance-and-health-care-prescription-drugs
- **Description:** Working papers from leading health economists on prescription drug markets, insurance design, and patient behavior. Pre-publication research on cutting-edge topics in drug pricing.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Economic analysis of price discrimination in drug markets
  - Research on patient response to cost-sharing mechanisms
  - Studies on information asymmetry in prescription decisions
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Theoretical foundation for understanding patient decision-making under uncertainty; economic rationale for price transparency

## Category 4: Government Resources & Databases

### 16. CMS Drug Spending Dashboard
- **URL:** https://www.cms.gov/Research-Statistics-Data-and-Systems/Statistics-Trends-and-Reports/Information-on-Prescription-Drugs
- **Description:** Public data on Medicare Part D spending, utilization, and pricing trends. Includes drug-level spending data, manufacturer pricing, and geographic variation. Updated annually with detailed CSV downloads available.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Drug-level spending and utilization for Medicare population
  - Price changes over time for specific medications
  - Geographic variation in prescribing and costs
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Shows actual insurance spending patterns; baseline data for predicting Medicare costs

### 17. FDA Orange Book (Approved Drug Products with Therapeutic Equivalence Evaluations)
- **URL:** https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-approvals-and-databases/orange-book-data-files
- **Description:** Comprehensive database of approved drugs, patent information, and therapeutic equivalence ratings. Essential for identifying generic alternatives and bioequivalent products.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Identifies therapeutically equivalent generic alternatives
  - Patent expiration dates predict future generic availability
  - AB-rated generics are substitutable without prescriber approval
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Generic availability is primary driver of insurance tier placement; essential for predicting cost-saving alternatives

### 18. Medicaid Drug Rebate Program (MDRP) Data
- **URL:** https://www.medicaid.gov/medicaid/prescription-drugs/medicaid-drug-rebate-program/index.html
- **Description:** Public data on manufacturer rebates to Medicaid, covered outpatient drug lists, and state pharmacy benefit management. Includes Average Manufacturer Price (AMP) and Best Price data.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Shows net prices after rebates (typically 23% mandatory rebate)
  - State supplemental rebate agreements provide additional savings
  - Demonstrates price negotiation mechanisms
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Understanding rebate structures explains insurance plan incentives and formulary decisions

### 19. NADAC (National Average Drug Acquisition Cost) Database
- **URL:** https://data.medicaid.gov/Drug-Pricing-and-Payment/NADAC-National-Average-Drug-Acquisition-Cost-/a4y5-998d
- **Description:** Survey-based pricing benchmark representing pharmacies' actual acquisition costs. Updated weekly with pricing for generic and brand medications. Used by many state Medicaid programs for reimbursement.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Weekly updated pharmacy acquisition costs
  - Benchmark for "true" drug cost without markup
  - Shows cost basis for pharmacy reimbursement calculations
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** Baseline cost data for understanding pharmacy margins and insurance reimbursement; helps predict cash pay pricing

### 20. Hospital Price Transparency (340B Drug Pricing Program Data)
- **URL:** https://www.hrsa.gov/opa/index.html
- **Description:** Federal program requiring drug manufacturers to provide outpatient drugs at discounted prices to covered entities (safety-net hospitals, FQHCs). Public database of participating entities and covered drugs.
- **Key Insights:**
  - Shows up to 50% discount on outpatient drugs for qualifying entities
  - Demonstrates price discrimination in pharmaceutical markets
  - Important for understanding hospital-based prescribing costs
- **Insurance Relationship Impact:** 340B pricing creates different cost structures for different care settings; affects patient out-of-pocket for hospital prescriptions

## Key Findings for FullFill Application

### Predictable Cost Outcome Model Components

Based on this research, building predictable cost outcomes requires integrating data from multiple sources:

1. **Real-Time Pricing Data**
   - GoodRx, RxSaver, SingleCare APIs for cash pay benchmarks
   - NADAC for pharmacy acquisition costs
   - State-specific Medicaid pricing

2. **Formulary & Coverage Intelligence**
   - CMS Part D Plan Finder for Medicare population
   - CoverMyMeds formulary database for commercial/Medicaid
   - Express Scripts and major PBM formulary publications
   - NCPDP real-time benefit check (RTBC) integration

3. **Patient-Specific Factors**
   - Insurance type (Medicare, Medicaid, Commercial, Uninsured)
   - Deductible status (met vs. unmet)
   - Formulary tier and restrictions
   - Geographic location (state-level Medicaid variations)

4. **Alternative Cost Pathways**
   - Generic substitutions (FDA Orange Book)
   - Therapeutic alternatives (formulary preferred drugs)
   - Patient assistance programs
   - Cash pay vs. insurance comparison
   - 340B eligible facilities

5. **Utilization Management Predictions**
   - Prior authorization likelihood and approval rates
   - Step therapy requirements
   - Quantity limit policies
   - Pharmacy network restrictions

### Implementation Recommendations

1. **Multi-Source Price Aggregation**: No single source provides complete pricing. Aggregate cash pay (GoodRx, RxSaver), insurance estimates (RTBC), and government benchmarks (NADAC).

2. **Insurance Type as Primary Segmentation**: Cost prediction accuracy improves dramatically when segmented by coverage type due to fundamentally different pricing structures.

3. **Geographic Considerations**: State Medicaid variations and regional pharmacy pricing require location-based data.

4. **Formulary Integration is Critical**: Tier placement and restrictions are the strongest predictors of patient cost under insurance.

5. **Patient Assistance Program Database**: Significant cost reduction opportunity not captured in standard pricing tools; requires separate database.

6. **Predictive Model Training**: Academic research (Health Affairs, JAMA) provides evidence-based frameworks for predicting prescription abandonment and patient behavior based on cost thresholds.

7. **Transparency as Competitive Advantage**: Research shows 25-30% prescription abandonment due to cost surprise; providing upfront estimates at prescribing stage significantly improves outcomes.

## Research Limitations

1. Most pricing APIs require commercial partnerships and are not free/open access
2. Real-time benefit check (RTBC) requires integration with pharmacy benefit managers
3. Formulary data changes quarterly; requires ongoing updates
4. Patient assistance program eligibility is complex and individualized
5. Actual patient cost depends on deductible status, which providers often don't know
6. Specialty medications and biologics have unique coverage pathways not well-captured by standard tools
